# CHE 360: Chemical Engineering Thermodynamics
## Fall 2026 | Dr. Priya Ramaswamy | University of Maine

### Course Description
Application of thermodynamic principles to chemical engineering systems. Topics include the first and second laws, thermodynamic property calculations, equations of state, phase equilibrium, and power/refrigeration cycles.

### Prerequisites
- CHE 200 (Principles of Chemical Engineering)
- PHY 122 (Physics II)
- MAT 228 (Calculus III)

### Format
- 3x 50-minute lectures per week
- 1x 50-minute recitation per week
- Enrollment cap: 40
- ABET-accredited program (EAC)

### Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:
1. Apply the first and second laws of thermodynamics to open and closed systems (ABET SO-1)
2. Calculate thermodynamic properties using equations of state and generalized correlations (ABET SO-1)
3. Analyze vapor-liquid equilibrium for ideal and non-ideal systems (ABET SO-1, SO-6)
4. Design simple separation processes using phase equilibrium data (ABET SO-2)
5. Evaluate thermodynamic efficiency of power cycles and refrigeration systems (ABET SO-1, SO-2)
6. Work collaboratively on open-ended thermodynamic analysis problems (ABET SO-5)

### Weekly Schedule
| Week | Topic |
|------|-------|
| 1 | Review: energy balances, state functions, PVT behavior |
| 2 | First law for closed systems; internal energy |
| 3 | First law for open systems; enthalpy, steady-state flow |
| 4 | Second law; entropy, Carnot cycle |
| 5 | Entropy calculations; ideal gas, liquids, solids |
| 6 | **Midterm 1**; Thermodynamic property relations |
| 7 | Equations of state: ideal gas, van der Waals, Peng-Robinson |
| 8 | Generalized correlations; departure functions |
| 9 | Phase equilibrium: Gibbs phase rule, fugacity |
| 10 | VLE for ideal solutions: Raoult's law, bubble/dew point |
| 11 | **Midterm 2**; VLE for non-ideal solutions: activity coefficients |
| 12 | Chemical reaction equilibrium |
| 13 | Power cycles: Rankine, Brayton |
| 14 | Refrigeration cycles; thermodynamic analysis of real systems |
| 15 | Review; **Final Exam** |

### Known Gaps
- No process simulation software (Aspen Plus) despite industry standard
- All homework is hand calculation; no computational tools beyond calculator
- Group project uses textbook data, not real experimental/industrial data
- No connection to contemporary applications (heat pumps, carbon capture, renewable energy systems)
