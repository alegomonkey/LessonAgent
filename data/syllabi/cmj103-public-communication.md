# CMJ 103: Fundamentals of Public Communication
## Fall 2026 | Dr. Marcus Chen | University of Maine

### Course Description
An introduction to the principles and practices of effective public communication. Students develop skills in informative and persuasive speaking, audience analysis, research, organization, and delivery through multiple speech performances.

### Prerequisites
None (General Education requirement)

### Format
- 3x 50-minute meetings per week
- Enrollment cap: 30 per section, 4 sections offered
- Gen-ed category: Written and Oral Communication (WOC)

### Learning Outcomes
Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:
1. Construct and deliver an organized, evidence-based oral presentation (WOC)
2. Analyze audience and adapt communication strategies accordingly (WOC)
3. Apply ethical reasoning to communication contexts (ETH secondary)
4. Demonstrate effective use of visual aids and supporting materials (WOC)
5. Evaluate peer presentations using constructive feedback criteria (CTCE secondary)
6. Manage communication apprehension through preparation and practice
