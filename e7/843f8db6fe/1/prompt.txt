BIO316 Assignment: Primary Literature Analysis & Experimental Design
Assignment Title:
Interpreting Primary Research in Molecular & Cellular Biology

Overview
In this assignment, you will critically analyze a peer‑reviewed research article in molecular or cellular biology and design a follow‑up experiment that builds on the study’s findings. This assignment assesses your ability to interpret data, evaluate methodology, and apply scientific reasoning to propose novel research.

Learning Objectives
By completing this assignment, you will be able to:

Interpret and evaluate experimental data from primary scientific literature

Identify strengths and limitations in experimental design

Synthesize findings into a coherent scientific narrative

Propose a logical, feasible follow‑up experiment

Communicate scientific ideas clearly in written form

Assignment Instructions
Part 1 — Article Selection (10%)
Choose one primary research article published within the last 5 years in one of the following journals:

Cell

Nature

Science

Journal of Cell Biology

Molecular Cell

PNAS

The article must include original data (not a review, commentary, or perspective).

Submit the article PDF with your assignment.

Part 2 — Article Summary (30%)
Write a 1–2 page summary addressing:

Biological question:  
What problem or hypothesis is the study addressing?

Background context:  
What is already known? Why is this question important?

Experimental approach:  
Identify the major techniques used (e.g., Western blotting, RNA‑seq, CRISPR, microscopy).

Key findings:  
Summarize the major results in your own words.

Conclusions:  
What do the authors claim, and how do the data support these claims?

Part 3 — Data Interpretation (30%)
Choose two figures from the article and analyze them in detail:

For each figure:

Describe the experimental setup

Identify independent and dependent variables

Explain the controls used

Interpret the data (what does the figure show?)

Evaluate whether the authors’ interpretation is justified

Your analysis should demonstrate deep engagement with the data.

Part 4 — Proposed Follow‑Up Experiment (30%)
Design a follow‑up experiment that logically extends the study.

Your proposal must include:

Rationale:  
Why is this experiment the next logical step?

Hypothesis:  
A clear, testable statement.

Experimental design:

Methods and techniques

Controls

Sample size or replicates

Variables

Predicted results:  
What outcomes do you expect?

Interpretation:  
How would each possible result support or refute your hypothesis?

Potential limitations:  
Identify at least one limitation and how you would address it.
