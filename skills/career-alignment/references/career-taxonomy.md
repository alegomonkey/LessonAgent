# Career Taxonomy Reference

This reference defines career clusters relevant to higher education curricula, organized by domain. Each cluster includes specific roles, required competency areas, typical employers, and connections to university programs. Use this taxonomy to normalize freeform student career goals into structured categories for alignment analysis.

---

## Cluster 1: Computing and Information Technology

### Software Engineering

**Specific Roles**: Front-End Developer, Back-End Developer, Full-Stack Developer, Mobile Developer, DevOps Engineer

**Required Competency Areas**: Data structures and algorithms, software design patterns, version control (Git), testing and quality assurance, CI/CD pipelines, at least two programming languages, database fundamentals, API design, agile methodologies, technical communication

**Typical Employers**: Technology companies (large and startup), financial institutions, healthcare IT firms, government agencies, defense contractors, consulting firms

**Connection to University Programs**: Computer Science (BS/MS), Software Engineering (BS), Information Systems (BS). Core courses include data structures, algorithms, software engineering, databases, and operating systems.

### Data Science

**Specific Roles**: Data Analyst, Data Scientist, Machine Learning Engineer, Business Intelligence Analyst, Quantitative Analyst

**Required Competency Areas**: Statistics and probability, programming in Python and/or R, SQL and database querying, data visualization, experiment design, machine learning fundamentals, data wrangling and cleaning, communication of findings to non-technical audiences, domain knowledge in at least one application area

**Typical Employers**: Technology companies, financial services, healthcare analytics firms, marketing and advertising agencies, government statistical offices, research institutions

**Connection to University Programs**: Data Science (BS/MS), Statistics (BS/MS), Computer Science (BS/MS), Applied Mathematics (BS/MS). Core courses include statistics, machine learning, databases, and data visualization.

### Systems Engineering

**Specific Roles**: Systems Administrator, Cloud Engineer, Site Reliability Engineer, Network Engineer, Infrastructure Architect

**Required Competency Areas**: Operating systems internals, networking protocols (TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP), cloud platforms (AWS, Azure, GCP), scripting and automation, monitoring and observability, security fundamentals, capacity planning, incident response

**Typical Employers**: Cloud providers, managed service providers, large enterprises with internal IT, telecommunications companies, government agencies

**Connection to University Programs**: Computer Science (BS), Information Technology (BS), Computer Engineering (BS). Core courses include operating systems, networking, systems programming, and cybersecurity.

### Cybersecurity

**Specific Roles**: Security Analyst, Penetration Tester, Security Engineer, Incident Responder, Compliance Analyst

**Required Competency Areas**: Network security, cryptography, threat modeling, vulnerability assessment, security frameworks (NIST, ISO 27001), forensics, scripting for automation, risk assessment, regulatory compliance, ethical hacking principles

**Typical Employers**: Cybersecurity firms, financial institutions, government and military agencies, healthcare organizations, consulting firms

**Connection to University Programs**: Cybersecurity (BS/MS), Computer Science (BS), Information Technology (BS). Core courses include network security, cryptography, ethical hacking, and digital forensics.

### ML/AI Research

**Specific Roles**: Research Scientist, Applied ML Engineer, NLP Engineer, Computer Vision Engineer, AI Ethics Researcher

**Required Competency Areas**: Linear algebra and calculus, probability and statistics, deep learning frameworks (PyTorch, TensorFlow), experiment design, research methodology, technical writing, domain expertise in at least one subfield (NLP, vision, reinforcement learning), software engineering fundamentals

**Typical Employers**: Research labs (academic and corporate), AI-focused startups, technology companies with research divisions, government research agencies

**Connection to University Programs**: Computer Science (MS/PhD), Applied Mathematics (MS/PhD), Electrical Engineering (MS/PhD). Core courses include machine learning, deep learning, linear algebra, and research methods.

## Cluster 2: Engineering

### Chemical Engineering

**Specific Roles**: Process Engineer, Environmental Engineer, Materials Engineer, Pharmaceutical Engineer, Energy Systems Engineer

**Required Competency Areas**: Thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, heat and mass transfer, reaction engineering, process design and simulation (Aspen Plus), safety and environmental compliance, materials science, laboratory techniques, technical communication

**Typical Employers**: Chemical manufacturers, pharmaceutical companies, oil and gas companies, environmental consulting firms, food and beverage companies, semiconductor manufacturers

**Connection to University Programs**: Chemical Engineering (BS/MS). Core courses include thermodynamics, transport phenomena, reaction engineering, and process design.

### Electrical Engineering

**Specific Roles**: Circuit Design Engineer, Power Systems Engineer, Embedded Systems Engineer, Signal Processing Engineer, Telecommunications Engineer

**Required Competency Areas**: Circuit analysis, electromagnetics, signal processing, control systems, embedded programming (C/C++), power systems, PCB design, MATLAB/Simulink, semiconductor physics, instrumentation

**Typical Employers**: Semiconductor companies, power utilities, telecommunications firms, defense contractors, consumer electronics manufacturers, automotive companies

**Connection to University Programs**: Electrical Engineering (BS/MS), Computer Engineering (BS). Core courses include circuits, electronics, signals and systems, control systems, and electromagnetics.

### Mechanical Engineering

**Specific Roles**: Design Engineer, Manufacturing Engineer, Thermal/Fluids Engineer, Robotics Engineer, Automotive Engineer

**Required Competency Areas**: Statics and dynamics, thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, materials science, CAD software (SolidWorks, AutoCAD), finite element analysis, manufacturing processes, machine design, project management, GD&T

**Typical Employers**: Automotive manufacturers, aerospace companies, manufacturing firms, energy companies, consulting engineering firms, robotics companies

**Connection to University Programs**: Mechanical Engineering (BS/MS). Core courses include statics, dynamics, thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, materials, and machine design.

### Civil Engineering

**Specific Roles**: Structural Engineer, Transportation Engineer, Geotechnical Engineer, Water Resources Engineer, Construction Manager

**Required Competency Areas**: Structural analysis, soil mechanics, hydraulics, transportation planning, construction management, environmental regulations, surveying, CAD and BIM software, project estimation, professional licensing (FE/PE exam preparation)

**Typical Employers**: Civil engineering firms, government agencies (DOT, EPA, Army Corps), construction companies, consulting firms, utilities

**Connection to University Programs**: Civil Engineering (BS/MS). Core courses include structural analysis, geotechnical engineering, hydraulics, transportation, and construction management.

### Biomedical Engineering

**Specific Roles**: Medical Device Engineer, Clinical Engineer, Biomechanics Researcher, Tissue Engineering Researcher, Regulatory Affairs Specialist

**Required Competency Areas**: Biology and physiology, biomechanics, biomaterials, medical device design, FDA regulatory knowledge, signal processing (biomedical signals), imaging systems, statistics for clinical studies, ethics in biomedical research

**Typical Employers**: Medical device companies, hospitals, pharmaceutical companies, research universities, regulatory agencies, biotechnology startups

**Connection to University Programs**: Biomedical Engineering (BS/MS), Electrical Engineering (BS) with biomedical focus. Core courses include biomechanics, biomaterials, physiology, medical imaging, and biomedical instrumentation.

## Cluster 3: Business and Management

### Product Management

**Specific Roles**: Associate Product Manager, Product Manager, Technical Product Manager, Growth Product Manager

**Required Competency Areas**: Market analysis, user research, data-driven decision making, agile/scrum methodology, roadmap planning, stakeholder communication, basic SQL and analytics tools, A/B testing, competitive analysis, prioritization frameworks

**Typical Employers**: Technology companies, SaaS companies, e-commerce companies, financial technology firms, healthcare technology companies

**Connection to University Programs**: Business Administration (BS/MBA), Information Systems (BS), Computer Science (BS) with business minor. Core courses include marketing, strategy, statistics, and project management.

### Marketing Analytics

**Specific Roles**: Marketing Analyst, Digital Marketing Specialist, SEO/SEM Analyst, CRM Analyst, Marketing Data Scientist

**Required Competency Areas**: Statistics, A/B testing, SQL, data visualization, marketing principles, customer segmentation, attribution modeling, marketing automation tools, communication and presentation, Python or R for analysis

**Typical Employers**: Marketing agencies, technology companies, retail companies, financial services, media companies, consulting firms

**Connection to University Programs**: Marketing (BS), Business Analytics (BS/MS), Statistics (BS). Core courses include marketing principles, statistics, data analysis, and consumer behavior.

### Consulting

**Specific Roles**: Management Consultant, Strategy Consultant, Technology Consultant, Operations Consultant

**Required Competency Areas**: Problem structuring, quantitative analysis, presentation and communication, Excel modeling, industry knowledge, project management, client relationship management, research and synthesis, teamwork under pressure

**Typical Employers**: Management consulting firms, strategy boutiques, Big Four professional services, internal strategy teams at large corporations

**Connection to University Programs**: Business Administration (BS/MBA), Economics (BS), Engineering (BS) with business minor. Core courses include strategy, finance, statistics, and organizational behavior.

### Finance

**Specific Roles**: Financial Analyst, Investment Banking Analyst, Risk Analyst, Portfolio Analyst, Corporate Finance Associate

**Required Competency Areas**: Financial accounting, corporate finance, financial modeling (Excel/VBA), valuation methods, statistics, economics, Bloomberg/Capital IQ, presentation skills, regulatory awareness, Python for quantitative finance

**Typical Employers**: Investment banks, asset management firms, insurance companies, corporate finance departments, fintech companies, private equity firms

**Connection to University Programs**: Finance (BS/MS), Economics (BS), Accounting (BS), Mathematics (BS). Core courses include financial accounting, corporate finance, investments, and econometrics.

## Cluster 4: Communication and Media

### Public Relations

**Specific Roles**: PR Specialist, Communications Coordinator, Media Relations Manager, Corporate Communications Analyst, Crisis Communications Specialist

**Required Competency Areas**: Writing for media, press release development, media relations, social media management, crisis communication, event planning, brand messaging, analytics and measurement, stakeholder management, ethical communication

**Typical Employers**: PR agencies, corporate communications departments, nonprofit organizations, government agencies, healthcare organizations

**Connection to University Programs**: Communication (BS), Journalism (BS), Marketing (BS). Core courses include public relations, media writing, strategic communication, and media law.

### Journalism

**Specific Roles**: Reporter, Investigative Journalist, Data Journalist, Multimedia Journalist, Editor

**Required Competency Areas**: News writing, interviewing, fact-checking, data analysis for reporting, multimedia production (audio/video), digital publishing, media law and ethics, deadline management, source development, beat reporting

**Typical Employers**: Newspapers, digital news outlets, broadcast media, wire services, investigative nonprofit newsrooms, trade publications

**Connection to University Programs**: Journalism (BS), Communication (BS), English (BS). Core courses include news writing, reporting, media law, editing, and multimedia journalism.

## Cluster 5: Healthcare and Life Sciences

### Nursing

**Specific Roles**: Registered Nurse, Nurse Practitioner, Clinical Nurse Specialist, Public Health Nurse, Nurse Educator

**Required Competency Areas**: Anatomy and physiology, pharmacology, patient assessment, clinical procedures, evidence-based practice, patient communication, electronic health records, team collaboration, ethical decision making, critical thinking in clinical settings

**Typical Employers**: Hospitals, clinics, long-term care facilities, public health departments, schools, home health agencies

**Connection to University Programs**: Nursing (BSN/MSN). Core courses include anatomy, physiology, pharmacology, nursing fundamentals, and clinical rotations.

### Biotechnology Research

**Specific Roles**: Research Associate, Lab Technician, Bioinformatics Analyst, Quality Control Analyst, Regulatory Affairs Associate

**Required Competency Areas**: Molecular biology, cell biology, laboratory techniques (PCR, gel electrophoresis, chromatography), bioinformatics, statistical analysis, GLP/GMP compliance, technical writing, data management, safety protocols

**Typical Employers**: Pharmaceutical companies, biotech startups, academic research labs, government research agencies (NIH, CDC), contract research organizations

**Connection to University Programs**: Biology (BS/MS), Biochemistry (BS/MS), Biomedical Engineering (BS). Core courses include molecular biology, biochemistry, genetics, and research methods.

### Clinical Informatics

**Specific Roles**: Clinical Informatics Specialist, Health Data Analyst, EHR Implementation Specialist, Health IT Project Manager

**Required Competency Areas**: Health information systems, database management, interoperability standards (HL7, FHIR), data analysis, clinical workflows, privacy and security (HIPAA), project management, user training, clinical terminology (ICD, SNOMED)

**Typical Employers**: Hospitals, health systems, EHR vendors, health IT consulting firms, government health agencies, insurance companies

**Connection to University Programs**: Health Informatics (BS/MS), Information Systems (BS), Nursing (BSN) with informatics focus. Core courses include health informatics, databases, project management, and health data analytics.

## Cluster 6: Education

### K-12 Teaching

**Specific Roles**: Elementary Teacher, Secondary Teacher (by subject), Special Education Teacher, STEM Educator, Instructional Coach

**Required Competency Areas**: Pedagogy, curriculum design, classroom management, assessment design, differentiated instruction, educational technology, student development, content expertise in teaching area, communication with parents and administrators, state certification requirements

**Typical Employers**: Public school districts, private schools, charter schools, international schools, tutoring organizations

**Connection to University Programs**: Education (BS/MEd), subject-area majors with teacher certification. Core courses include educational psychology, methods courses, assessment, and student teaching.

### Higher Education

**Specific Roles**: Professor, Lecturer, Academic Advisor, Curriculum Coordinator, Educational Developer

**Required Competency Areas**: Deep subject expertise, course design, research methodology, grant writing, academic publishing, mentoring, assessment of learning outcomes, accreditation processes, educational technology, service and committee work

**Typical Employers**: Universities, community colleges, research institutions, professional development organizations

**Connection to University Programs**: Doctoral programs in any discipline, Higher Education Administration (MEd/EdD). Core courses include graduate coursework in the discipline plus teaching practica.

### Instructional Design

**Specific Roles**: Instructional Designer, Learning Experience Designer, E-Learning Developer, Training Specialist, Curriculum Developer

**Required Competency Areas**: Learning theory (constructivism, behaviorism, connectivism), instructional design models (ADDIE, SAM), LMS administration, multimedia development, assessment design, user experience principles, accessibility (WCAG), project management, stakeholder collaboration

**Typical Employers**: Universities (online learning departments), corporate training departments, e-learning companies, government training agencies, healthcare education

**Connection to University Programs**: Instructional Design (MS), Education Technology (MS), Communication (BS) with ID focus. Core courses include instructional design, learning theory, multimedia development, and program evaluation.
